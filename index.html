One topic I find interesting is doing timing in Rowing, when getting Accurate times in rowing it can often be hard as the start is anywhere from 1 to 5 kilometers (0.6 miles to 3.2 miles) away from the finish. This means methods such as having one timer are harder to do. Traditionally you have atleast two stopwatches. The first stopwatch records the time on the water from all boats starting to the first boat finishing. The second one records the gaps between boats after the first one finishes. This requires a lot of math and can be imprecise due to differing visual markers and dead even races. Nowadays more modern technology such as ScanLine Cameras combined with dedicated companies such as https://www.crewtimer.com/ and https://clockcaster.com/ allow for more precise timing and faster distrobution of timing to spectators.
